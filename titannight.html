Chess Club

×

Advisor: Adam Schmierer

Student Leader(s): Wesley Li

Meetings are Friday, Weekly in Rm 232, 1st lunch and Rm 227 2nd lunch

Chess Club is a student run, informal chess club at Union High School that normally happens at lunch. The goal of the club is to teach people how to play chess and get new people into the game. It is also a time for people to play with each other in a fun atmosphere. Some students are very competitive and will play each other in a much more formal way. Through this others are exposed to how competitive chess is played while connecting with others on campus who enjoy the game.

Book Club

×

Advisor: Dell Morey

Student Leader(s): Madeline Edwards, Alana Chariarse, and Heaven Mekonen

Meetings are every second Tuesday of the month at 3:15 after school in the library.

Book Club is a fun and easy way to get involved at school! We get to read a variety of different books and discuss them together (with your input highly valued). We also eat snacks, play games, and if certain books have movies made after them, we try and watch those too! Book club is also sensitive to Union's hard-working students, so no high commitment is required. Anyone from book lovers to new readers is welcome! Members can join at any point during the year when we start a new book (every month). We like to be sensitive to students' homework and testing times so members can come and go as they like. If a member does receive the book for that month, it is assumed they will join us for our next meeting to discuss it. However, there is no penalty administered.

National Spanish Honor Society

×

Advisor: Tammy Kirby

Student Leader(s): Vanessa Utama & Alexia Bravo

Meetings are Wednesday, Bi-weekly in Rm 136, 3:15pm

The National Spanish Honors Society is an organization that inspires its members to connect with their peers, their community, and themselves through club meetings and volunteer opportunities. This experience allows participants to gain knowledge about Spanish and the Spanish-speaking community with capstone projects, teach others about the beauty and the Spanish language during tutoring, and assist the Spanish-speaking community via volunteer opportunities. Speech and Debate

×

Advisor: Ms. Schroff

Student Leader(s): Ayden Erickson, Elliot Holden & Alexia Bravo

Meetings are Monday, weekly in Rm 133, 3:15-4:15pm

Do you like acting? What about stand-up comedy? Arguing about what you care about? Then join the speech and debate team! Speech is typically individual events where you present an idea or an issue to a group of people, and debate is usually a discussion between people on two opposing sides. Regardless of which you choose, speech and debate is highly competitive and requires an extreme amount of dedication, collaboration, and hard work. But don’t be afraid! You aren’t doing this alone- your speech and debate team will help you through the entire process, from the first draft to national competitions.

Voters of Tomorrow

×

Advisor: Christopher Ramage

Student Leader(s): Ayden Erickson

Meetings are Wednesday, bi-weekly in Rm 402, 3:15-3:45pm

Last year, voter turnout in Clark County was 36%- that means that only 36% of eligible voters in the entire county actually voted! As future voters, we’re trying to change that. Voters of Tomorrow is all about getting students of all ages involved in local politics. Whether that means hosting voter registration drives or meeting local politicians, we’re a bipartisan group that’s all about getting YOUR voice heard. This means getting people registered to vote, writing letters to local politicians, and meeting the politicans in our county. Regardless of political belief, we believe getting involved is the first step towards a better tomorrow.
